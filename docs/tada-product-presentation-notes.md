# TaDa! Product Presentation Notes

## Slide 1
Open with the product promise: TaDa! is presentation mode for HTML. It is especially useful now that AI tools create lots of polished HTML.

## Slide 2
Frame the shift. AI tools now create useful HTML artifacts, but the handoff to a client presentation is still clumsy.

## Slide 3
Make the pain obvious. Browsers are good for browsing, but not for a client presentation.

## Slide 4
Introduce TaDa! as the bridge: it is not an editor, generator, or design tool. It is the presentation layer for HTML.

## Slide 5
Explain ingest breadth. This is the first user-facing friction point, so the deck should make it feel flexible and forgiving.

## Slide 6
Talk through the normalizer. This is what makes TaDa! feel magic: it does not require one perfect HTML template.

## Slide 7
Use the Ely deck as the concrete sample. It is important to show real content, not theoretical UI.

## Slide 8
Show what the presenter actually gets: a clean stage, active slide rail, and the sample content rendered correctly.

## Slide 9
This is the performance proof slide. Emphasize that the earlier bottleneck was thumbnail rebuilds, and this is now fixed.

## Slide 10
Explain the standalone show idea. This is the closest equivalent to PowerPoint's self-running or exported show experience.

## Slide 11
Position the roadmap and production shape: desktop first, mac build available, Electron gives cross-platform path.

## Slide 12
Close with the core message. If the audience remembers one thing, it should be that TaDa! is presentation mode for HTML.
